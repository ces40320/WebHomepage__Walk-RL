Abstract

Predictive musculoskeletal simulation provides a powerful framework for studying human locomotion, but learning physically realistic control policies remains challenging due to the high dimensionality of muscle activations and limited experimental data.

We introduce a physiology‑informed reinforcement learning framework that constrains control using muscle synergy priors derived from inverse musculoskeletal simulation.

By embedding neurophysiologically meaningful structure into the policy representation, the proposed approach improves biomechanical fidelity while maintaining the flexibility of reinforcement learning.

Across multiple walking speeds and slopes, the synergy‑constrained controller produces joint kinematics, ground reaction forces and muscle activation patterns that more closely match experimental observations compared with unconstrained controllers.

These results demonstrate that incorporating muscle synergy structure into reinforcement learning enhances realism and generalization in predictive musculoskeletal locomotion simulations.
